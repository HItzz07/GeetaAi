# Bhagavad Gita Query Matching Guide

This guide helps match common user queries to appropriate Bhagavad Gita verses.

## Common Query Categories and Matching Verses

### 1. Grief, Loss, Mourning
- **Query examples**: "I lost someone", "grieving", "passed away", "heartbroken"
- **Best matches**: 
  - 2.11: Grief is natural but wisdom recognises that lamenting over what cannot be changed only deepens suffering
  - 2.20: The soul is never born nor dies at any time
  - 2.27: For death is as sure for that which is born, as birth is for that which is dead

### 2. Anxiety, Fear, Worry
- **Query examples**: "anxious", "worried", "scared", "fear of failure"
- **Best matches**:
  - 2.47: You have a right to perform your prescribed duties, but you are not entitled to the fruits of your actions
  - 2.48: Perform your duties being steadfast in yoga, abandoning attachment
  - 6.5: Let a person lift themselves up by their own self

### 3. Purpose, Meaning, Direction
- **Query examples**: "what is the point", "life feels meaningless", "no direction"
- **Best matches**:
  - 9.22: To those who are constantly devoted to serving Me with love, I carry what they lack
  - 9.27: Whatever you do, whatever you eat, whatever you offer in sacrifice—do that as an offering to Me
  - 18.66: Abandon all varieties of duty and simply surrender unto Me

### 4. Anger, Frustration, Rage
- **Query examples**: "angry", "frustrated", "lost my temper", "rage"
- **Best matches**:
  - 2.63: From anger, complete delusion arises
  - 3.37: It is desire—and it is anger—born of the mode of passion
  - 16.3: Anger is one of the three gates to hell

### 5. Self-worth, Identity, Confidence
- **Query examples**: "worthless", "not good enough", "who am i", "identity crisis"
- **Best matches**:
  - 2.17: Know that which pervades the entire body is indestructible
  - 2.20: The soul is never born nor dies at any time
  - 3.35: It is better to perform one's own duty imperfectly than to perform another's duty perfectly

### 6. Work, Career, Burnout
- **Query examples**: "burned out", "working too hard", "no motivation", "exhausted"
- **Best matches**:
  - 3.9: Work done as a sacrifice becomes liberating
  - 3.19: Therefore, without being attached to the fruits of activities, one should act as a matter of duty
  - 6.17: He who is regulated in eating, recreation, work, and sleep can mitigate all material pains

### 7. Relationships, Love, Connection
- **Query examples**: "relationship problems", "lonely", "breakup", "feel alone"
- **Best matches**:
  - 12.13: One who is not envious but is a kind friend to all living entities
  - 12.15: One who does not disturb others and is not disturbed by the world
  - 9.22: To those who are constantly devoted to serving Me with love

### 8. Knowledge, Learning, Clarity
- **Query examples**: "confused", "need clarity", "what should i do", "seeking answers"
- **Best matches**:
  - 4.38: In this world there is nothing as sublime and pure as transcendental knowledge
  - 4.39: A faithful person who is dedicated to knowledge gains that knowledge
  - 10.10: To those who are constantly devoted to serving Me with love, I give the understanding

### 9. Patience, Acceptance, Letting Go
- **Query examples**: "cant let go", "need patience", "acceptance", "letting go"
- **Best matches**:
  - 2.14: The contacts between the senses and sense objects give rise to feelings... They come and go; they are impermanent
  - 2.47: You have a right to perform your prescribed duties, but you are not entitled to the fruits
  - 18.47: It is better to engage in one's own duty even though imperfectly

### 10. Courage, Strength, Resilience
- **Query examples**: "need strength", "feeling weak", "scared to fail", "lack courage"
- **Best matches**:
  - 2.38: Treating alike happiness and distress, gain and loss, victory and defeat
  - 11.3: Never did there exist a time when I did not exist, nor you, nor all these kings
  - 18.58: If you become conscious of Me, by My grace you will pass over all obstacles

## How to Use This Guide

1. Identify the core emotion or need in the user's query
2. Match it to the appropriate category above
3. Select one of the suggested verses
4. Consider the context - sometimes multiple verses may apply
5. For best results, combine 2-3 verses that address different aspects of the query

## Example Implementation

In an application, you could:
1. Pre-process verses with thematic tags (as in the original file)
2. Create a mapping from query keywords to verse IDs
3. Use semantic matching for more nuanced queries
4. Rank results by relevance to the specific query context
